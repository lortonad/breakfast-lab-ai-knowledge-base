# Competitor notes: PeakBook (working doc, pasted from everywhere)

Compiled by Maya, various dates. CAUTION: sources range from "their
actual pricing page" to "a guy told Dale at a trade show." Marked where
I remembered to.

## From their pricing page (checked June 20, 2026)

- Starter $149/mo, Growth $289/mo, Pro $499/mo
- ALL tiers charge a 1.9% booking fee on top. Buried in the FAQ, not on
  the pricing table. This is the thing customers hate. Screenshot saved
  somewhere. (Find it. TODO.)
- Growth tier is what Torch Lake Paddle is on — matches Dale's "289 a
  month" exactly, so his numbers are reliable.

## From public stuff

- Founded 2019, Boulder CO. Started as ski/climbing gym booking, moved
  into "outdoor experiences" generally.
- An outdoor industry trade piece from late 2024 said "over 4,000
  operators." That number is two years old — treat as stale. Their site
  now just says "thousands of operators," which either means it grew
  past specifics mattering or shrank enough to hide. Can't tell which.
- Job listings (checked July 1, 2026): hiring in enterprise sales and
  a "Hotels & Resorts" vertical lead. Zero listings mentioning waivers,
  inventory, or anything small-operator. Read: they're moving upmarket.
  That's interpretation, not fact, but the listings are real.

## Secondhand / rumor tier

- Dale (June 18 interview): PeakBook went down "most of a Saturday
  morning" in August 2025. One customer's account, no incident report
  found. Believe the pain, verify the details before repeating.
- A guy at the Grand Rapids outdoor rec expo told Dale that PeakBook is
  raising a round to push into hotels. THIRD-hand by the time it reached
  this doc. Do not repeat this anywhere. Filed under: plausible,
  unverified, consistent with the job listings though.
- Bianca (first call, June 26): said PeakBook's waiver "integration" is
  actually just a partnership link-out to a separate product with a
  separate login, and her staff refuses to use it. ONE data point, but
  it's from actually using it, so weight it above the trade-show guy.

## What I actually conclude (opinion, mine)

1. They will not fix waivers or multi-location inventory for small
   operators, because their org chart points at hotels.
2. Their booking fee is our best marketing copy and we didn't have to
   write it.
3. If the upmarket move is real, small liveries get MORE neglected over
   the next 18 months, not less. The window is open.

## Anti-goals

Do not become obsessed with PeakBook. They are not the competition.
Clipboards are the competition. Dale's real alternative to us was never
PeakBook-but-better, it was Katie typing waivers at 9pm.
